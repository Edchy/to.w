## Design Context

### Users
Primarily people discovering or following the artist's work — friends, potential buyers, anyone drawn to handcraft and jewelry. Not a webshop yet; the site exists to show the work and establish a presence. Browsing is unhurried, personal. The viewer should feel like they've been let into the artist's world, not a storefront.

### Brand Personality
Human, imperfect, down to earth. TO.W makes jewelry and handcraft — physical, tactile, made by hand. The site should feel like it was made with the same care and directness as the objects themselves. Not polished into sterility. Quiet and contemplative, but alive.

Three words: **human · imperfect · grounded**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Light. Natural light, like objects on a table by a window.
- **Tone:** Quiet editorial. Generous whitespace. Photography carries the weight.
- **Reference:**  restrained palette, gallery-like spacing, work speaks for itself. Avoids trendy effects, excessive color, decorative noise.
- **Anti-references:** Cold luxury branding, techy dark mode, gradient accents, anything that feels like a fashion brand trying to look artisanal.
- **Color:** Not decided.
- **Typography:** NOT system fonts. Something with character — a serif or semi-serif with a handmade or editorial quality for display; a quiet, unpretentious sans for body. Must feel chosen, not defaulted to.

### Design Principles
1. **Let the work speak.** Images are the content. The UI is the frame, not the painting.
2. **Imperfection is honest.** Don't over-smooth. A slightly unexpected layout choice or weight contrast is more memorable than perfection.
3. **Warmth through restraint.** The site should feel warm not through color saturation, but through generous space, considered typography, and an unhurried pace.
4. **No unnecessary decoration.** Every visual element must earn its place. If it doesn't serve the work or the visitor, remove it.
5. **Tactile over digital.** Typography and spacing should evoke something physical — a printed zine, a tag on a piece of jewelry, a handwritten label.
